# Certification Research: CEH & CISSP

## 1. Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH)

### **Overview**
The Certified Ethical Hacker (CEH) certification is one of the most recognized credentials in the cybersecurity industry. Offered by EC-Council, this certification focuses on ethical hacking skills, including penetration testing and vulnerability assessment. It equips professionals to think like a hacker but act as a defender, helping to improve security measures.

### **Certification Details**
- **Provider**: EC-Council
- **Duration**: 2-3 months
- **Cost**: ₹60,000 – ₹1,00,000 (varies based on location)
- **Skills Covered**:
  - Hacking techniques and tools
  - Penetration testing
  - Risk analysis and mitigation strategies
  - Vulnerability assessment and security management

### **Alignment with SMART Goals**
- **Short-Term Goal**: Achieving CEH certification within the next year aligns with my short-term goal to gain hands-on experience in cybersecurity and understand hacking techniques.
- **Measurable Indicator**: Passing the CEH exam and gaining practical experience by working in ethical hacking internships.

## 2. Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP)

### **Overview**
The Certified Information Systems Security Professional (CISSP) is a globally recognized certification provided by (ISC)². It is targeted at professionals looking to advance in the field of information security. The CISSP certification covers a broad range of topics related to security management, risk management, and system architecture.

### **Certification Details**
- **Provider**: (ISC)²
- **Duration**: 6-12 months
- **Cost**: ₹1,00,000 – ₹1,50,000
- **Skills Covered**:
  - Security and risk management
  - Asset security
  - Security engineering and architecture
  - Network and telecommunications security
  - Identity and access management

### **Alignment with SMART Goals**
- **Medium-Term Goal**: To achieve the CISSP certification in the next 2-3 years as part of my medium-term goal to transition into roles such as Security Architect.
- **Measurable Indicator**: Earning the CISSP certification, followed by job responsibilities that involve leading cybersecurity teams in large-scale enterprises.

---

## 3. Conclusion
Both certifications are valuable for building a successful career in cybersecurity. CEH is best suited for professionals aiming to work in ethical hacking, while CISSP is essential for those pursuing leadership roles in information security.
